The first Santa Cruz event on the calendar this year was run over the
weekend of April 26/27. Warm sunny weather and light breezes were
supplied. Unfortunately on Saturday the westerly fought the easterly
with the out come in the balance for far too long. We had plenty of new
teams racing with several teams coming a long way to race, sorry we
could not provide more wind! We also had 2 Parkers racing for the first
time in a long time in California as well as the rebuilt "Fasterin Fog"
a 23 year old Kyrwood, all of these old steeds were aquiting them selves
well. In fact at times Steve and Peter in a mid '80's Parker were going
extremely well, especially with new sails on Sunday and Pierre and Paul
were fighting neck and neck with the latest and greatest in the Kyrwood.

Sunday was a much better days sailing, breeze up to the trapezeing end
of the spectrum, though never enough for wire running to pay off and
again another warm and sunny day. Once the dust had settled and the
scores added up The team of Nick, Andy and Steve won, Andy pulling the
light wind duty on Saturday, followed by Bruce and Cameron in second and
Mark and Arthur in third.

In our boat as it was Carl's last event before heading to China for
a year we swapped for the first race on Sunday. Obviously I did what all
good helms would do when presented with the front end of the boat,
I made sure I dragged in the water, let the kite flap and generally took
us in the wrong direction as much as possible, after all we don't need
any more crews thinking they are Mike Martin....

A big thank you to Aaron for having us over for a BBQ Saturday night and
to Carl for supplying a keg of beer for the weekend. Hopefully the wind
gods will supply more breeze for Treasure Island next weekend and the
So-Cal fleet doesn't have to get their weekly manicures at the weekend.

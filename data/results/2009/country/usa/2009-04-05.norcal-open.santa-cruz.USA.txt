Santa Cruz was a late stand in for what was scheduled to have been
a Treasure Island regatta over the April 4/5 weekend. We had an
impressive 18 boats on the start line with at least 7 countries being
represented on the water!

Saturday was sunny, cool and a good breeze blowing from the start,
probably around 12-15 all day. Plenty of swell running and a fair amount
of kelp on the course added to the entertainment. While most of us had
pretty close racing, Andy and Howie seemed to be in a different league
all day, winning all 4 races with some ease. Behind them was a chasing
group taking turns at the minor places. 

Following racing most of the competitors and many others attended
a birthday bash for Nick Adamson and Bob Tenant who were both turning
40. Many thanks to both of them for having us over.

Sunday looked like being a completely different day, with a forecast for
a light Southerly breeze. On the course the wind was in the usual
direction and blowing a just sailable 4 or so knots, bringing back some
unpleasant memories of Palermo for those of us who were there. A race
was started and at the first mark led convincingly by Guy and Andrew in
the newly acquired 8842. However, unfortunately for them with the wind
all but gone, the race was abandoned. The restart got under way in
a building 6-8 knots, with Morgan stepping in for Hasso with Pete coming
out on top ahead of Brian and Ted. What was to be the final race got
away in full trapezing conditions, which built to good wire running
conditions. Andy and Howie pulled through on the run to win the one lap
race from Jeff and Mike.

All weekend long the racing was very close, with lots of place changing
and tight mark roundings, very exciting and enjoyable. Next up is the
PCC's at Cabrillo Beach Yacht Club on April 25/26.

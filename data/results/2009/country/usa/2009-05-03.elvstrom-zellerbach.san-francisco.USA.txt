Aaron Ross
2009-05-04

Eight 5O5 teams competed in the  Elvstrom Zellerbach Regatta at St
Francis Yacht Club last weekend. The late invitation explained some of
the light attendance. And the weather, or was it swine flu, explains
a few more (Mike, Mike, Nick, Jeff, Bruce).

Winds out of the Southwest up and down from 8 to 12 with gusts with at
least one race closer to 15 knots Sunday. Boats were always trapezing
upwind, and the runs were split between sitting on the tanks and wiring.
The rain held off while sailing, and it only sprinkled in the mornings.

Howard Hamlin and Paul Cayard sailed fast and smart most of the time (go
figure), but there were some challenging shifts and holes at the weather
mark, making each team look smart and not so smart at various times.
Howard and Paul won the regatta, counting 5 bullets and dropping
a second place in the final race. Bob Tennant and Rich Mundell sailed
fast and mostly smart in Bob’s new Rondar, taking advantage of the drop
in race 3, when their spinnaker would not douse before the start. The
teething issues are getting sorted out, the boat has only been wet
4 days. Aaron Ross and Pat Diola sailed just fast and smart enough to
stay somewhat in the hunt, and ended the regatta in third.

Soon to be new owner Andrew Watters teamed up with Johnny Goldsberry in
a borrowed boat, and showed that talent, smarts, and maybe a little luck
can overcome tired sails and low tech equipment, taking a hard fought
win in race 6. They also almost lead around the first weather mark on
Saturday. Had it not been for a tape to tape spinnaker tear in race 2,
they likely would have finished on the podium. Anyone want to buy
a winning Kyrwood? Steve Khlea and Brian Haines teamed up for Sunday
only and showed good form, always in the hunt in 4 races.

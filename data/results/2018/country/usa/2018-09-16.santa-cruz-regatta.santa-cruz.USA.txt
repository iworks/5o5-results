Santa Cruz 505 Fall Open
Mike Holt
2018-09-17

What a fantastic weekend of 505 racing! Ten boats showed up in Santa
Cruz to be treated to a Saturday or “as good as it gets” racing. With
the wind high teens to low twenties from the off and a big swelling
running we had the perfect playground to play in. To no one’s huge
surprise Adam and Mike dominated the day with 3 bullets, Steve and Bob
fired in two seconds and Eb and Mike pulled off a 2nd and the death in
the 3rd race, apparently much to the disgust of Mr Bourdow…

A thorough debrief followed the racing with much learnt, but
unfortunately, you had to be there…

Sunday was looking to be a repeat of Saturday, but the wind was slower
to fill in, but once it did we had 2 more races with the wind in the
teens and big waves to add to the excitement. Adam and Mike again had it
their own way but were bushed hard first by Steve and Bob and then Reeve
and Jeff.

Sailing for the first time in a 505 and acquitting himself really well
was Andy Hamilton sailing with Pat Diola and all the way from Annapolis
to sample the delights of the Monterey Bay was Ali Meller, managing to
drag Mike Smith out of retirement and on to the water!

A great weekend as ever, well run by the Santa Cruz Yacht Club and the
many helpers there. Next up, Fall Dinghy at StFYC.
